### ðŸ“‹ Example: Faulty Framing
> Poor workmanship causes framing to collapse. **Resultant damage = covered**, but cost to redo the framing correctly = NOT covered.

> [!TIP] **Plain Dumb Down:** Building collapses because of bad work? Insurance pays for the damage. But you pay to FIX the bad work.

---

### Policy Period & Limits

| Feature |
|---------|
| Limit = completed value of construction |
| Starts: when construction starts |
| Ends: when building occupied OR left unattended >30 days |

### Premium Structure
- Premium based on max value of one project (materials + labour)
- Rates LOW at inception (less to insure)
- Rates FULL at completion (more to insure)
- Must report values at stated times
- Adjusted within 30 days of completion

### Per Project vs Blanket
| Per Project | Blanket |
|-------------|---------|
| 1 project = 1 COC | Multiple projects = 1 policy |

## ðŸ”§ Contractors Equipment Floater (26:14 - 27:32)

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (1574s):** Standard commercial property only covers equipment on premises. Floater covers movable equipment.

| Equipment Floater |
|------------------|
| Covers movable equipment (backhoes, loaders, power tools) |
| Owned, rented, or controlled by insured |
| Scheduled OR blanket basis |
| 30-day reporting requirement for new equipment |

### Coverage Includes
- Mechanical/electrical breakdown
- Temperature extremes
- Rust/corrosion
- Exceeding load limits

> [!TIP] **Plain Dumb Down:** Your tools go from job site to job site. Regular insurance doesn't cover them on the road. This floater does.
