### 2ï¸âƒ£ Private Companies ðŸ¢

Private insurers come in two main types:

#### A) Stock Companies ðŸ“ˆ

**Owned by shareholders, profit-oriented**

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | Shareholders (investors) |
| **Objective** | Generate profits for shareholders |
| **Capital** | Raised through stock markets |
| **Profits** | Paid as dividends or reinvested |

> ðŸ‡¨ðŸ‡¦ **Canadian Examples (2026):**
> - **Intact Financial** - Canada's largest P&C insurer (TSX: IFC)
> - **Manulife Financial**
> - **Sun Life Financial**
> - **Aviva Canada**
> - **TD Insurance**

#### B) Mutual Companies ðŸ¤

**Owned by policyholders (members), NOT profit-oriented**

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | Policyholders are called "members" |
| **Objective** | Collect just enough to cover losses & operations |
| **Profits** | Returned to members as dividends or lower premiums |
| **Losses** | Members may pay extra to cover shortfalls |

> ðŸ‡¨ðŸ‡¦ **Canadian Examples (2026):**
> - **Gore Mutual** - Oldest P&C insurer in Canada (merged with Beneva Jan 2026)
> - **The Co-operators** - Co-operative owned by 46 member organizations
> - **Portage Mutual**
> - **Cayuga Mutual**
> - **Trillium Mutual**

##### 4 Types of Mutual Companies:

| Type | Key Feature |
|------|-------------|
| **1. Assessment Mutual** | Policyholders are members, elect board, may be assessed for shortfalls |
| **2. Cooperative** | Shareholders are insured, contribute capital, surplus paid based on contribution |
| **3. Factory Mutual** | Focus on fire insurance & prevention, strict requirements (sprinklers, alarms) |
| **4. Stock Mutual** | Assessment mutual that transformed to insure general public, fixed premiums |

### 3ï¸âƒ£ Captive Insurers ðŸ”’

**Wholly owned and controlled by the insureds themselves.**

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | The companies being insured own it |
| **Purpose** | Self-insurance for large/related companies |
| **Operation** | Acts like traditional insurer but serves only its owners |
| **Benefits** | Customize coverage, retain profits, manage own risks |

> ðŸ’¡ **Example:** Air Canada could create its own captive insurer instead of buying from Intact - they insure themselves and keep any profits.

> ðŸ“ **2026 Canada:** Most Canadian-owned captives are domiciled in Barbados/Bermuda. British Columbia and Alberta have domestic captive legislation.

## ðŸ“Š Stock vs Mutual: Quick Comparison

| Feature | Stock Company | Mutual Company |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **Owned by** | Shareholders | Policyholders |
| **Goal** | Maximize profit | Serve members |
| **Profits go to** | Shareholders (dividends) | Members (lower premiums) |
| **Claims approach** | Standard | Often more lenient |
| **Premiums** | Market competitive | Often cheaper long-term |
| **Voting rights** | Shareholders vote | Members vote |
