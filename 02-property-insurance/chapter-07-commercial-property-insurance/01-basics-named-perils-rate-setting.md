### The 5 Factors That Affect Fire Rate

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (282.78s-353.42s):** "The first factor is building construction. A concrete building is cheaper to ensure than a wood frame building because it's more resistant to fire. They will also look at your claims history, both the claims history for your type of business and the claims history of the applicant himself or herself. It will look at your last five years. The third factor is protection against fire... Public protection means protection from outside sources... distance from a fire hydrant or fire hall... With private protection, that's protection installed by the applicant... sprinklers or alarms... The next thing they look at is occupancy... the type of business... And finally, it will look at your location... distance from other buildings..."

| # | Factor | What They Look At |
|---|--------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Building Construction** | Concrete = cheaper, wood frame = more expensive |
| 2 | **Claims History** | Business type history + your personal history (5 years) |
| 3 | **Protection (Public)** | Distance from fire hydrant/fire hall, water supply |
| 3 | **Protection (Private)** | Sprinklers, alarms (discounts available) |
| 4 | **Occupancy** | Type of business (yours AND neighbors) |
| 5 | **Location** | Area, distance from other buildings, crime rate, nearby building construction |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> **5 things that affect your fire rate:**
> 1. **What's it made of?** Concrete = safe. Wood = risky.
> 2. **Claims history?** Bad history (you or your industry) = higher rate.
> 3. **Protection?** Near a fire station? Have sprinklers? = discount.
> 4. **What business?** A restaurant (fire risk) costs more than an office.
> 5. **Where is it?** High crime area? Next to a gas station? = more expensive.
