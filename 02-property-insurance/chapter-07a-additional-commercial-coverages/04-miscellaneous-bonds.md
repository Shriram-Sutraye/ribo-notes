### Who Needs Fiduciary Bonds?

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Court-appointed administrators | Manage affairs on court's behalf |
| Guardians | Care for minors or incapacitated |
| Trustees | Manage trusts for beneficiaries |
| Executors of wills | Carry out deceased's wishes |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> A fiduciary is someone handling OTHER PEOPLE'S money or affairs. They have a "trust" position. The bond guarantees they won't steal or mess things up. Examples:
> - Handling a dead person's estate
> - Managing a child's inheritance
> - Being appointed by court to manage someone's affairs

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Grandma dies and leaves $1 million. Uncle Bob is the administrator. The estate administrator bond guarantees Uncle Bob will:
> - Follow grandma's will exactly
> - Not steal any money
> - Give everyone their fair share
> 
> If Uncle Bob steals, the bond pays the family back!

|------------------|

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Parents die in a car crash, leaving a 10-year-old with $500,000 inheritance. Aunt Jane becomes the guardian. The guardianship bond guarantees:
> - Aunt Jane uses the money FOR THE CHILD (not herself)
> - She keeps proper records of every dollar spent
> - She follows the court's instructions
> 
> If Aunt Jane spends the money on herself, the bond pays to protect the child!
