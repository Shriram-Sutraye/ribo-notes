## ðŸ’° Part 8: Basis of Claim Payment (9:16-11:16)

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (555.64s-578.28s):** "The basis of claim payment here would typically be the damage to the building if there is any, plus the damage to personal property if there is any, minus your deductible. ### Claim Payment Formula:

| Formula |
|---------|
| Building Damage + Property Damage - Deductible = Payout |
| Deductible applied **ONCE** to total loss |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Your house has $10,000 damage and your stuff has $5,000 damage. Total loss = $15,000. With a $1,000 deductible, you get $14,000. You do NOT pay $1,000 twice!

### Dwelling & Structures: Replacement Cost vs ACV

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (579.16s-602.6s):** "Dwellings and detached private structures are insured on a replacement cost basis or if you elect not to repair them then you get actual cash value. | Basis of Settlement | Condition |
|---------------------|-----------|
| **Replacement Cost** | If you repair/rebuild |
| **Actual Cash Value** | If you choose NOT to repair |

**Repair Requirements:**
- Materials of similar quality
- Same location
- Same occupancy
- Reasonable time

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> If your house burns down and you REBUILD, you get full replacement cost (brand new). If you say "forget it, I'm moving to a condo," you only get ACV (depreciated value).
