## 1Ô∏è‚É£ Definition of Insurance

> **Insurance** = An undertaking (agreement) by one party (insurer) to indemnify (compensate) another (insured) if a specific peril (danger/risk) damages an object of insurance (the thing being covered).

### The 5 Key Elements (MEMORIZE THIS):

| # | Element | Simple Explanation | Example |
|---|---------|---------------------|---------|
| 1 | **Transfer Risk** | You pay an insurer to take on your risk | You pay $100/month so if your house burns, THEY pay, not you |
| 2 | **Promise to Pay** | Insurer promises to pay when a **specific peril** (covered danger) damages the **object of insurance** (covered thing) OR when you cause loss to others | Fire damages your house = insurer pays. You hit someone's car = insurer pays them |
| 3 | **Accidental Losses Only** | Only pays for **unexpected** losses that happen **after** the policy starts | ‚úÖ Lightning strike. ‚ùå You set your own house on fire |
| 4 | **Principle of Indemnity** | Payment = Amount of Loss based on value **immediately before** the loss. No profit! | Your 5-year-old TV was worth $300 before loss = you get $300, not $800 (original price) |
| 5 | **Money or Repair/Replace** | Insurer can pay cash, repair the item, OR replace it | Car accident: insurer can pay you cash OR send it to a shop |

> üí° **Why Accidental Only?** If insurance paid for intentional losses, people would burn their own stuff for money. That's fraud!

## 2Ô∏è‚É£ Functions of Insurance

Insurance serves **5 major functions** in society:

### Overview:

| Function | What It Means | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Spread Risk** | From few to many | Most important function! |
| **Loss Prevention/Reduction** | Insurers help reduce losses | Saves money for everyone |
| **Peace of Mind** | Eliminates worry | Lets people take risks |
| **Supply Credit** | Lenders require insurance | Can't get a mortgage without it |
| **Source of Jobs & Capital** | Insurers invest premiums | Huge industry, lots of jobs |

---

### üìå Spread Risk (MOST IMPORTANT FUNCTION)

> **Concept:** Premiums from MANY people are pooled together to pay losses of the FEW who actually have claims.

#### How It Works:

| Without Insurance | With Insurance |
|-------------------|----------------|
| You alone pay $500,000 for your house fire | 100,000 people each pay $500/year |
| Financial ruin! | Pool of $50 million pays the few who have fires |

> üí° **Think of it like:** Everyone chips in $5 to a pizza fund. If you get hungry, the fund buys you pizza. Most people don't get hungry, but those who do are covered.

---

### üìå Loss Prevention/Reduction

Insurers actively help **prevent and reduce losses**:

| How Insurers Help | Example |
|-------------------|---------|
| Send reps to visit sites | Inspector visits your factory, suggests sprinklers |
| Offer discounts for safety | 10% off for home alarm system |
| Partner with communities | Campaigns against insurance fraud |
| Nationwide safety programs | Fire prevention awareness |

> üí° **Why do they do this?** Fewer claims = more profit for insurer. It's a win-win!

---

### üìå Peace of Mind

> **Concept:** You pay a **small fixed amount** (premium) to fend off **large uncertain losses** (claims).

| Without Insurance | With Insurance |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Constant worry: "What if my house burns?" | Relaxed: "I'm covered, no big deal" |
| Can't take risks: "I'm scared to start a business" | Can take risks: "If it fails, I'm protected" |

> üí° **This enables entrepreneurship!** People are more willing to start businesses, buy property, and invest.

---

### üìå Supply Credit

> **Concept:** Lenders (banks) won't lend money for **unprotected property** ‚Äî you must show **evidence of insurance** to close the deal.

| Situation | What Happens |
|-----------|--------------|
| Buying a house with mortgage | Bank requires home insurance BEFORE closing |
| Financing a car | Dealer requires auto insurance BEFORE driving off |
| Business loan for equipment | Bank may require equipment insurance |

> üí° **Why?** If the house burns down, the bank loses their collateral. Insurance protects THEM too!

---

### üìå Source of Jobs & Capital

Insurers **invest the premiums** they collect:

| What Insurers Do | Impact |
|------------------|--------|
| Invest in bonds, stocks, real estate | Provides capital for economy |
| Employ thousands of people | Underwriters, adjusters, agents, brokers |
| Ontario P&C alone | 125,000+ jobs |

> üí° **Premiums don't sit in a vault** ‚Äî they're invested and working in the economy until needed for claims.

## 3Ô∏è‚É£ General Insurance (P&C) Categories

RIBO covers **General Insurance**, also called **Property & Casualty (P&C)**:

| # | Category | Description | Size |
|---|----------|-------------|------|
| 1 | **Automobile** | Personal & commercial auto | **#1 Largest** (required by law!) |
| 2 | **Property** | Personal & business property, fire, theft | **#2 Second largest** |
| 3 | **Liability** | Protection if you're legally responsible for injuring someone or damaging their property | Also called "Casualty" |

> ‚ùå **NOT Covered by RIBO:** Life Insurance (requires separate LLQP license)

### What's "Liability" Insurance?

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Liability** (also called Casualty) | Pays when you're **legally responsible** for causing injury or damage to a **3rd party** (someone else) |

> **Example:** You accidentally back into your neighbor's car. Your **liability coverage** pays for THEIR repairs.

## 4Ô∏è‚É£ Types of Insurers

There are **two main categories**: Government and Private

| Category | Key Feature |
|----------|-------------|
| **Government** | Usually compulsory (mandatory) |
| **Private** | Stock or Mutual companies |

---

### üìå Government Insurance

| Program | What It Is |
|---------|------------|
| **EI (Employment Insurance)** | Pays if you lose your job |
| **Provincial Health Plans (OHIP)** | Covers hospital, doctors |
| **Workers' Compensation (WSIB)** | Workplace injury coverage |
| **CMHC** | Mortgage default insurance |

> üí° These are typically **compulsory** (mandatory) ‚Äî you don't choose to buy them.

---

### üìå Private Insurance: Stock Companies

**Definition:** Owned by **shareholders** & **profit-oriented**

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | Shareholders (investors who buy stock) |
| **Capital Source** | Private funds OR public stock issuance |
| **Goal** | Make profit for shareholders |
| **Profits go to** | Reinvested OR paid as dividends to shareholders |
| **Coverage** | Writes MOST policies, licensed in most provinces |
| **Examples** | Intact, Aviva, Travelers |

> üí° **Key Difference:** Profits go to SHAREHOLDERS, not policyholders.

---

### üìå Private Insurance: Mutual Companies

**Definition:** Owned by **policyholders (members)** & **NOT profit-oriented** (collects premium to cover costs only)

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | Policyholders are members (you're an owner!) |
| **Goal** | Cover costs, not make profit |
| **Premiums** | Often cheapest |
| **Claims adjusting** | Usually more lenient (lax) |
| **If company loses money** | Policyholders may pay the difference |
| **If surplus (extra money)** | Paid to policyholders as dividend, reinvested, OR kept in reserve |
| **Examples** | Gore Mutual, Wawanesa, Cayuga Mutual |

> üí° **Key Difference:** Profits go to POLICYHOLDERS, not external shareholders.

---

### The 4 Types of Mutual Companies:

#### 1. Assessment Mutual

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Structure** | Every policyholder is a member & elects a board |
| **Premiums** | Go into communal fund for claims |
| **Administration** | Employs a secretary to run it |
| **How Payment Works** | Insured signs a **premium note** (max amount payable). Pays an **initial premium** (portion). If claims exceed fund, insurer can request the **uncalled portion** (the rest). |

> üí° **Simple:** You agree to pay UP TO $1,000/year. You pay $600 upfront. If claims are bad, they can ask for the other $400.

#### 2. Cooperative Mutual

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Structure** | Shareholders are the insureds & contribute capital |
| **Voting** | Each shareholder gets 1 vote |
| **Surplus** | Paid out based on amounts contributed |
| **Premiums** | **Non-assessable** (they can NOT ask you to pay more) |

> üí° **Key Difference from Assessment:** They CAN'T come back and ask for more money!

#### 3. Factory Mutual

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Focus** | Fire insurance & **prevention** |
| **Requirements** | Strict fire protection BEFORE insuring |
| **Inspections** | Detailed inspections conducted |
| **How It Works** | Insured pays a **deposit premium** for losses, expenses & reserve |
| **At End of Term** | Deposit returned OR credited towards renewal |
| **Bonus** | May get credit for investment gains of the reserve |

> üí° **Think of it like:** Industrial/manufacturing focus. They're strict about safety but may refund part of your premium.

#### 4. Stock Mutual

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **What It Is** | An Assessment Mutual that did a **legal transformation** to insure the public |
| **Premiums** | **Fixed at inception** (start) ‚Äî not subject to adjustments |
| **No surprises** | They can't charge more if they make or lose money |

> üí° **Hybrid:** Started as mutual but now operates more like a stock company with fixed premiums.

---

### üìå Captive Insurance Company

**Definition:** Insurer is **wholly owned & controlled by its insureds** (the companies being insured).

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | Owned by the insureds themselves |
| **How It Works** | Operates like traditional insurer, puts own money at risk |
| **Benefits** | Keeps any profits, controls coverage |
| **Who Uses It** | Large related companies needing risk financing |
| **Purpose** | Self-insure hard-to-place or expensive risks |

> üí° **Example:** A group of 5 construction companies create their own captive insurer to cover high-risk projects. They fund it themselves and keep the profits.

---

## üìù Quick Comparison: Stock vs Mutual vs Captive

| Feature | Stock | Mutual | Captive |
|---------|-------|--------|---------|
| **Owned by** | Shareholders | Policyholders | Parent company/group |
| **Goal** | Profit | Cover costs | Self-insure |
| **Profits go to** | Shareholders | Policyholders | Parent company |
| **Can ask for more $?** | No | Sometimes (Assessment) | N/A |
| **Coverage** | Public | Public | Private (own group) |

## üß† Practice Questions

### Question 1:
> A mutual insurance company has a surplus at the end of the year. What happens to the surplus?
> 
> a) Paid to shareholders as dividends  
> b) Paid to policyholders as dividends or kept in reserve  
> c) Returned to the government  
> d) Donated to charity

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: b) Paid to policyholders as dividends or kept in reserve**

Mutual companies are owned by **policyholders**. Any surplus is either:
- Paid to policyholders as dividends
- Reinvested in the company
- Kept in reserve for future claims

</details>

---

### Question 2:
> An Assessment Mutual is running low on funds to pay claims. What can they do?
> 
> a) Nothing, they must dip into reserves  
> b) Call the uncalled portion of the premium note from policyholders  
> c) Increase premiums for next year only  
> d) File for bankruptcy

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: b) Call the uncalled portion of the premium note from policyholders**

Assessment Mutuals have policyholders sign a **premium note** with a maximum payable amount. They pay an **initial premium** upfront. If claims exceed the fund, the mutual can request the **uncalled portion** (the remainder up to the max).

**Key:** This is what makes Assessment Mutuals unique ‚Äî you may have to pay more!

</details>

---

### Question 3:
> What is the MOST important function of insurance?
> 
> a) Providing peace of mind  
> b) Spreading risk from few to many  
> c) Supplying credit to lenders  
> d) Creating jobs and capital

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Answer: b) Spreading risk from few to many**

Risk spreading is the **fundamental purpose** of insurance. By pooling premiums from many people, the losses of the few can be paid. Without this function, individual loss would be catastrophic.

</details>

## üéì Key RIBO Takeaways

1. **Insurance = Risk transfer** ‚Äî you pay premiums, insurer takes on your risk
2. **Only accidental losses** are covered (after policy starts)
3. **Indemnity = No profit** ‚Äî you're restored to pre-loss position only
4. **Spread Risk = #1 Function** ‚Äî premiums from many pay losses of few
5. **Stock = Shareholders** | **Mutual = Policyholders**
6. **Assessment Mutuals CAN ask for more money** ‚Äî Cooperatives CANNOT
7. **Captives = Self-insurance** for large groups
8. **Auto is #1 largest P&C category** ‚Äî required by law in Ontario!
