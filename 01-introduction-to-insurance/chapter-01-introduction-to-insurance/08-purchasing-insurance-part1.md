### Step 4ï¸âƒ£: Get Client Consent (Express or Implied)

> ðŸ’¡ **From Screenshot:** "Get client consent (express or implied) before proceeding"

**This is required under PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)!**

#### Two Types of Consent:

| Type | Description | When Required |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| **Express Consent** | Clear, direct agreement (signature, checkbox) | Sensitive info (credit, health, financial) |
| **Implied Consent** | Assumed based on context | Non-sensitive, obvious uses |

> ðŸŽ¯ **Plain English:**
>
> **Express Consent** = "I explicitly agree"
> - Signing a form
> - Checking a box
> - Saying "yes, I consent"
>
> **Implied Consent** = "It's obvious I agree"
> - If you're applying for car insurance, it's implied you consent to them checking your driving record
> - BUT for sensitive stuff (credit checks, health info), you need EXPRESS consent

#### What Makes Consent "Meaningful"?

| Requirement | What It Means |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Clear** | No legal jargon, plain language |
| **Specific** | Exactly what info, exactly why |
| **Informed** | Client understands consequences |
| **Voluntary** | Can withdraw anytime |

#### Sensitive Information (Express Consent Required):

- âœ… Credit reports/scores
- âœ… Health information
- âœ… Financial data
- âœ… Claims history
- âœ… Anything outside "reasonable expectations"

> ðŸŽ¯ **Plain English:** If you're going to run a credit check, you CANNOT just assume it's okay. You must:
> 1. TELL the client you're doing it
> 2. Explain WHY you need it
> 3. Get their explicit "yes"

### 2ï¸âƒ£ Written Applications

> ðŸ’¡ **From Screenshot:** "Written - Benefits: 1. Accurate & checklist, 2. Recommendations & fewer errors, 3. Evidence of coverage, better service, & legal disclosures"

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|

#### 3 Key Benefits of Written Applications:

| Benefit | What It Means | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|----------------|

| **2. Recommendations & Fewer Errors** | Forces structured approach | Reduces mistakes and omissions |
| **3. Evidence of Coverage, Better Service & Legal Disclosures** | Creates paper trail | Protects broker AND client if disputes arise |

> ðŸŽ¯ **Plain English - Why Written is Better:**
>
> **Scenario:** Client has a claim denied because they didn't have flood coverage.
>
> | With Oral App | With Written App |
> |---------------|------------------|
> | Client: "They never asked about flood!" | You have signed form showing flood was offered and declined |
> | Broker: "I definitely asked!" | Clear evidence of what was discussed |
> | Result: He said/she said dispute | Case closed - documentation speaks |
>
> **The paper trail protects everyone.**
