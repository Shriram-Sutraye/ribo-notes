## âš–ï¸ Part 2: Large vs Small Loss Principle

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (1127s-1168s):** "Another critical concept to selecting and implementing the right risk management techniques is understanding the large versus small loss principle. So when it comes to a large loss, something with a devastating loss potential, you likely want to transfer that via insurance. When it comes to small losses, those can often be retained because it's cheaper to retain them and pay for them out of pocket than buying insurance for them."

### The Principle:

| Loss Size | Best Method | Why |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| **Large** (devastating) | **Transfer** (insurance) | Can't afford to pay yourself |
| **Small** (minor) | **Retain** (pay yourself) | Cheaper than insurance |

---

### ðŸ“‹ Transcript Example: Combination Approach

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (1151s-1168s):** "Typically, you would use a combination of the two. For example, you would purchase property insurance to take care of the big things like fire and earthquake, but then you would put a large deductible on it so then you could retain small losses like vandalism or small sort of damage."

| Strategy | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Property insurance** | Covers fire, earthquake |
| **Large deductible** | You pay first $5,000-$10,000+ |
| **Result** | Big losses = insured, Small losses = retained |

> ðŸŽ¯ **Plain English:** Get insurance for the big stuff (fire, earthquake), but use a high deductible so you pay for small stuff (vandalism, minor damage) yourself. Best of both worlds!
