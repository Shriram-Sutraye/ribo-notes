## ðŸ“ Part 2: Policy Changes

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript:** "Outside of processing new business or renewals, we also might be asked from time to time to assist customers with policy changes or remarketing."

### What is a Policy Change?

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript:** "A policy change involves adding or removing coverage, adjusting limits, or changing the named insured. You can use things like endorsements, riders to make amendments to the policy."

| Policy Change Type | Examples |
|--------------------|----------|

| **Removing coverage** | Drop collision on old car |

| **Changing named insured** | Adding spouse, removing ex |

### How Changes Are Made:

| Method | What It Is |
|--------|------------|
| **Endorsement** | Amendment to the policy |
| **Rider** | Additional coverage attached |
| **OPCF (Auto)** | Ontario Policy Change Form |

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript:** "When it comes to auto insurance, they're called OPCFs and we'll learn more about that in the auto insurance section."

> ðŸŽ¯ **Plain English:** 
> - **Endorsement** = A change to the policy (adding coverage, changing limits)
> - **Rider** = Extra coverage "riding along" with the base policy
> - **OPCF** = Specifically for auto insurance in Ontario (e.g., OPCF 44 for rental cars)

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript:** "This can happen for a few reasons."

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript:** "Or it can also be done upon suggestion of the broker for the same reasons. You say, hey, you know what? We haven't shopped it around in a while. Let's shop it to see if we can find you some better pricing."
