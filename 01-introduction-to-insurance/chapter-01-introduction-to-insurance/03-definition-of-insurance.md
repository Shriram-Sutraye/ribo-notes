**The agreement between the insurance company and the insured to transfer risk.**

- Insurance is a **contract** that allows the insured to transfer risk to the insurance company
- If anything happens, it's the **insurance company that has to pay**, not you
- You're essentially shifting your financial exposure to the insurer

> ðŸ’¡ **Example:** If your house burns down, the insurance company bears the financial burden, not you.

---

## 2ï¸âƒ£ Promise to Pay

**The insurance company promises to pay when:**

1. A **specific peril** damages an **object of insurance**
   - Example: Fire damages your house
2. When the insured **causes loss to other people** 
   - This is called **liability insurance** (covered later)

---

## 3ï¸âƒ£ Accidental Losses Only

**Insurance will ONLY pay for accidental losses that happen AFTER the agreement starts.**

### âŒ What's NOT Covered:
- Things that happened **before** you bought insurance
- Things you do **on purpose** (intentional acts)

> âš ï¸ **Key Point:** You cannot buy fire insurance while your house is already on fire and expect coverage!

---

## 4ï¸âƒ£ Principle of Indemnity

**The amount of payment you get for a claim equals the amount of loss based on the value of the object immediately prior to the loss.**

### What This Means:
- You get paid based on the **actual value** at the time of loss
- Factors like **depreciation** are considered
- You're made "whole" again, not given extra money to profit

> ðŸ”‘ **Purpose:** Prevents people from profiting from insurance claims - you're restored to your pre-loss financial position, nothing more.

### Principle of Indemnity - Real-World Application

The principle of indemnity is a **cornerstone of P&C (Property & Casualty) insurance** in Canada:

- Aims to restore the insured to the **exact financial position before the loss** - no more, no less
- Helps **prevent insurance fraud** by ensuring no one profits from a claim
- **Exception:** "Rebuilding Cost Guarantee" coverage can extend beyond strict indemnification when actual rebuilding costs exceed property value

### Ontario Auto Insurance Reforms (July 1, 2026)

A major change affecting the "Promise to Pay" component:

- **Before:** Bundled system with mandatory accident benefits
- **After:** **Modular system** - consumers can choose which benefits to include
- Previously mandatory benefits (income replacement, caregiver support) become **optional**

> [!CAUTION]
> Risk of **underinsurance** if consumers opt out of essential coverage without understanding implications.
