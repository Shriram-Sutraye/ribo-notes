## ðŸŽ¯ Part 3: Risk Management Goal

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (160s-189s):** "The goal of risk management is to reduce the financial consequences of the loss or reduce the probability of loss happens in the first place. > ðŸŽ¯ **Plain English:** Weigh the cost of insurance vs. the cost of the loss. For small stuff like shoplifting, maybe just accept the loss. For big stuff like fire, definitely get insurance!

### Three Elements of a Loss Exposure

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (287s-329s):** "When it comes to a loss exposure, there's really three elements of it. The first is the potential cause of loss or damage. So for example, it could be fire, cyber attack, theft, terrorist attack, something of that nature. The second thing you want to look at for a loss exposure are the financial consequences of an occurrence. So if this was to occur, what would the financial consequences be? | Element | What to Identify |
|---------|------------------|
| **1. Cause of loss** | Fire, cyber attack, theft, terrorist attack |
| **2. Financial consequences** | How much money would we lose? |
| **3. Objects/items at risk** | What can be exposed to loss? |

### ðŸ“‹ Transcript Example: Building

|---------|---------|

### Why Each Matters:

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (354s-392s):** "The loss history is important because you can see historically what types of losses the business has sustained.
