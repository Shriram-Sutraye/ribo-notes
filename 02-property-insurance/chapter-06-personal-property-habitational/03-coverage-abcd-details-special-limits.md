> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (702.12s-734.6s):** "When it comes to personal property it's again done on a replacement cost basis but certain items are only valid for actual cash value. ### Personal Property: RC vs ACV

| Settlement | When Applied |
|------------|--------------|
| **Replacement Cost** | Default for most items |
| **Actual Cash Value** | Fine art, antiques, memorabilia |

### Items Limited to ACV:

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (719.0s-734.6s):** "If you damage a Mona Lisa there is no replacement Mona Lisa right, it's a one-of-a-kind thing. Also if the property is not well maintained, it's no longer being used for its original purpose, for example if you have a laptop and now you're using it as a door stop, or if property is not replaced within 180 days."

| ACV-Only Items |
|----------------|
| Fine art |
| Antiques |
| Memorabilia |
| Property not well maintained |
| Property not used for original purpose |
| Property not replaced within 180 days |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Regular stuff (TV, couch, clothes) = Replacement Cost (you get a NEW one).
> Unique stuff (Mona Lisa, grandma's antique ring) = ACV only (because there's no replacement).
> Your old laptop you use as a doorstop = ACV only (it's not being used as a laptop).
> You have 180 days to buy a replacement, or you get ACV instead.

---

### 180-Day Replacement Option

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (735.32s-746.68s):** "So what you can do as an insured is you can opt for actual cash value first but then you have 180 days to change your mind back to replacement cost."

| 180-Day Rule |
|--------------|
| Can accept ACV first |
| Have 180 days to switch to RC |
| Must replace the item within 180 days |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Not sure if you want to replace that old TV? Take the ACV check now. If you change your mind and buy a new TV within 180 days, come back and they'll give you the difference to make it replacement cost.

---

### Media & Books of Account

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript (746.68s-775.959s):** "When it comes to media, they only pay the cost to re-copy from duplicates so they won't pay you to actually recreate that media again. And for books of account they will only pay for the cost of blanks so buying those books again and then the cost to re-copy or transcribe. So again similar to media not the cost to actually recreate those records."

| Item | What's Covered |
|------|----------------|
| **Media** | Cost to re-copy from duplicates (not recreation) |
| **Books of Account** | Cost of blank books + cost to transcribe |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Your family photos on a hard drive are destroyed. Insurance pays to copy them from a backup, NOT to hire someone to recreate them from memory.
> Your accounting ledgers are destroyed. Insurance buys you new blank books and pays to copy from backups, NOT to hire a forensic accountant to figure out what was in them.
