### Injury vs Sickness

> üí° **From Transcript (1016.16s-1020.72s):** "Body injury is something that comes from external sources. It's usually physical like any hit by a car."

> üí° **From Transcript (1020.72s-1025.6s):** "Sickness is something that manifests internally, such as developing cancer after consuming poison water."

> üí° **From Transcript (1025.6s-1030.88s):** "Death means death resulting from either bodily injury or sickness."

| Type | Source | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Injury** | External | Hit by car (physical) |
| **Sickness** | Internal | Cancer from poison water |
| **Death** | From either | Result of injury or sickness |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> - **Injury:** Something from the OUTSIDE hurts you (car crash, fall)
> - **Sickness:** Something goes wrong INSIDE you (poisoning causes cancer)
> - **Death:** The ultimate result of either

## üè† Property Damage (17:15 - 17:27)

> üí° **From Transcript (1030.88s-1038.16s):** "Property damage means physical injury to tangible property including the loss of use to undamaged property."

| Property Damage = |
|------------------|
| Physical injury to **tangible property** |
| PLUS: Loss of use of **undamaged property** |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Property damage covers:
> - The thing you broke (the crashed car)
> - AND the use they lost (can't use the car while it's being fixed)
> 
> Even if you didn't damage something directly, if they can't USE it because of what you did, that counts!

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Key Characteristic: Legal Liability Required (17:27 - 17:42)

> üí° **From Transcript (1038.16s-1044.16s):** "An important characteristic of liability policies is that they only pay when the insured is found legally liable."

> üí° **From Transcript (1044.16s-1049.6s):** "That means the insured has to lose in court before the liability policy will pay damages."

| KEY CHARACTERISTIC |
|-------------------|
| Only pay when insured is **legally liable** |
| Must LOSE in court first |
| Then policy pays damages |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Liability insurance only kicks in if you're found AT FAULT. Unlike property insurance where you just report a loss, liability insurance waits until a court says "yes, you owe them money." Then your insurance pays.

### Nominal Damages

> üí° **From Transcript (1077.52s-1083.04s):** "Normative or nominal damages are awarded when there is liability but there's no actual damage."

> üí° **From Transcript (1083.12s-1090.24s):** "In that case, the court would order the defendant to pay the plaintiff $1 or some other nominal sum to determine who was right and who was wrong."

| Nominal Damages |
|----------------|
| Liability exists but NO actual damage |
| Court orders tiny payment ($1) |
| Purpose: Determine right vs wrong |
| **NOT insurable!** |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Sometimes you were WRONG but didn't actually HURT anyone. Court says "pay them $1" just to officially say you were wrong. Insurance doesn't cover this‚Äîthere's no real loss to pay for!

---

### Punitive/Exemplary Damages

> üí° **From Transcript (1090.24s-1097.36s):** "Exemplary or punitive damages exist to punish the defendant."

> üí° **From Transcript (1097.36s-1103.76s):** "This could include fines or other aggravated damages."

| Punitive Damages |
|-----------------|
| Purpose: PUNISH the defendant |
| Includes fines, aggravated damages |
| **NOT insurable!** |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Punitive damages = "We're punishing you for being really bad!" The court wants to make an example of you. Insurance CAN'T cover this‚Äîif it did, there'd be no punishment! You pay this from your own pocket.

---

### Compensatory Damages

> üí° **From Transcript (1103.76s-1111.44s):** "Compensatory damages are amounts meant to compensate the wrong party for their injuries. This is the only type of damage that is insurable."

| Compensatory Damages |
|--------------------|
| Compensate the victim for injuries |
| **ONLY type that is insurable!** |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Compensatory damages = "This is what it actually COSTS to make things right." Medical bills, lost wages, repair costs‚Äîthe real expenses. THIS is what liability insurance pays!

### Accident vs Long-term Exposure

> üí° **From Transcript (1149.44s-1155.52s):** "An accident is defined as something that is sudden and unexpected and occurs at a particular point in time such as a car crash."

> üí° **From Transcript (1155.52s-1161.6s):** "It continues or long-term exposure occurs over a long period of time, such as being exposed to contaminated baby powder for a number of years."

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Accident** | Sudden, unexpected, specific time | Car crash |
| **Long-term Exposure** | Happens over years | Contaminated baby powder |

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> Two kinds of occurrences:
> - **Accident:** BOOM! Car crash at 3pm on Tuesday (sudden, specific moment)
> - **Long-term exposure:** Using contaminated baby powder for 10 years and developing cancer (gradual, over time)
> 
> Both count as "occurrences" for insurance purposes.
