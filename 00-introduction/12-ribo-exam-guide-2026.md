## ğŸ“– Open-Book Resources

The RIBO exam is **open-book**, but you are limited to a specific **Consolidated PDF** provided during the digital exam. 

### What's IN the PDF:
- **OAP 1:** Ontario Automobile Policy (Owner's Form)
- **RIB Act:** Registered Insurance Brokers Act
- **RIBO By-Laws:** By-Laws No. 1, 2, and 3
- **Ontario Regulations:** (308/98, 989, 990, 991)

### What's NOT in the PDF:
- âŒ **Habitational Policy Wordings:** You must memorize the core coverages and exclusions for home insurance as the HAB booklet was removed.
- âŒ **Your own notes:** No physical or digital personal notes are allowed.

## ğŸš€ 2026 Success Strategies

1. **Master the PDF Navigation:** Don't waste time scrolling. Know exactly where the RIB Act vs. OAP 1 sections are located in the consolidated document.
2. **Focus on Auto (OAP 1):** 25% of the exam is Auto. The OAP 1 is in your open-book PDF; learn to find information there quickly.
3. **Practice Application:** Use mock exams that give you scenarios (e.g., "A client does X, which exclusion applies?") rather than just definitions.
4. **Time Management:** You have roughly 1.5 minutes per question. If a question is too hard, flag it and move on.
5. **Ethics & By-laws:** These are "easy" points if you know the RIB Act. They often fall under General Insurance (25%).
