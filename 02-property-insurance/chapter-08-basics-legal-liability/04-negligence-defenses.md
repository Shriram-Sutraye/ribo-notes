### Factor 2: Plaintiff Contributed

> ğŸ’¡ **From Transcript (433.52s-439.92s):** "If the plaintiff's actions somehow led to the situation or contributed to it."

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> If YOU partly caused your own injury, you can't fully blame the other person. Example: You were jaywalking when a car hit you. Sure, the driver should've been careful, but YOU shouldn't have been jaywalking! Your negligence contributed to the accident.

### Factor 3: Deliberate Action by Plaintiff/Third Party

> [!TIP]
> **Plain Dumb Down Version:**
> If YOU (the plaintiff) did something deliberate that caused the harm, or if someone ELSE (third party) jumped in and caused the harm, then the original defendant can't be fully blamed. The chain of events was BROKEN by someone else's deliberate act.
