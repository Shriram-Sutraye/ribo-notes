### Examples of Financial Obligations

> ðŸŽ¯ **Plain English:** An insurance company is "solvent" if they have enough money to:

## ðŸ¤ Part 4: Fiduciary Responsibilities

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript:** "In the insurance industry, everyone is considered a fiduciary because a fiduciary is someone who handles other people's money."

### What is a Fiduciary?

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Fiduciary** | Someone who handles OTHER PEOPLE'S money |

> ðŸŽ¯ **Plain English:** When you pay your car insurance premium, that money isn't the broker's or insurer's to spend freely. They're holding it IN TRUST for a specific purpose. That makes them fiduciaries.

---

### Broker Fiduciary Responsibilities

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript:** "The fiduciary responsibility of a broker is to hold commissions in trust and return any unearned commissions if the policy should be cancelled part-way. An Ontario broker will remit premiums to the insurance company every 30 to 60 days, as well as hold that money in trust."

| Broker Duty | What It Means |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Hold commissions in trust** | Keep commission money in separate trust account |
| **Return unearned commissions** | If policy cancelled, give back what you didn't earn |
| **Remit premiums every 30-60 days** | Send collected premiums to insurer regularly |
| **Hold premiums in trust** | Client's money in trust account until remitted |

> ðŸŽ¯ **Plain English:** As a broker:
> - You can't mix client premiums with your personal money
> - You must send premiums to the insurer within 30-60 days
> - If a policy is cancelled, you must return any commission you didn't earn

---

### Insurer Fiduciary Responsibilities

> ðŸ’¡ **From Transcript:** "The fiduciary responsibility of the insurance company is to return any unearned premiums."

| Insurer Duty | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Return unearned premiums** | Refund the portion of premium not yet "used" |
